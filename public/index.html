Our Mission

To educate and increase the proficiency of its members concerning scientific advancement and progress in the science of amateur radio communications. To organize and train licensed radio amateurs capable of maintaining radio communications as a public service during periods of emergency. To encourage and sponsor experimental activities in radio communications and electronics, to the end that skills and experience gained in amateur radio will further the application of electronics to the benefit of the public at large.

Emergency Communications

Supporting Van Wert County and surrounding areas during storms, disasters, and community events with reliable, independent radio communication.

Community Events & Planning

Partnering with local agencies for parades, festivals, and preparedness drills — providing logistics and communication support.

Skywarn & Weather Spotting

As trained spotters, our members relay real-time reports to the National Weather Service during severe weather events.

Radio Training & Licensing

We help newcomers earn their FCC amateur radio license and mentor them as they get on the air. No experience needed!

CERT & Volunteer Integration

Many of our members serve with Community Emergency Response Teams (CERT), ARES, and similar groups — combining skills for greater impact.

Technical Projects & Repeaters

From maintaining local repeaters to building antennas, digital nodes, and solar-powered stations — we love radio tech.

About us

The Van Wert Amateur Radio Club (W8FY) is a community of licensed amateur radio operators serving Van Wert County and surrounding areas. Our mission is to promote the art and science of radio communication through public service, emergency preparedness, technical education, and on-air activities. We welcome newcomers, support local events, and maintain reliable communication resources like repeaters and nets. Whether you're a seasoned ham or just getting started, W8FY has a place for you!
